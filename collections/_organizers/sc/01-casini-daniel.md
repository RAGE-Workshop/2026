bio: >-
    Daniel Casini is a Tenure-Track Assistant Professor at the Real-Time Systems (ReTiS) Laboratory of the Scuola Superiore Sant’Anna of Pisa. He graduated (cum laude) in Embedded Computing Systems Engineering from the Scuola Superiore Sant’Anna of Pisa and the University of Pisa, and received a Ph.D. in computer engineering at the Scuola Superiore Sant’Anna of Pisa (with honors). He was a visiting scholar at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems (Germany) in 2019. His research interests include software predictability in multi-processor systems, computer architectures, embedded systems, schedulability analysis, distributed systems, operating systems, virtualization, security, and safe and secure artificial intelligence. He has been a technical program committee member in several international conferences, including RTSS, RTAS, ECRTS, and EMSOFT. He has been the technical program chair of SIES 2024, ISORC 2025, and DSD 2025. He received several awards at international conferences and workshops, including RTAS and DATE. He is an editorial board member of Elsevier’s Microprocessors and Microsystems and Springer’s Journal of Supercomputing. In 2024 and 2025, he has been recognized in the “World’s Top 2% Scientists” ranking by Stanford University and Elsevier.
